Self-therapy

Self-therapy

The use of self-help strategies for mental well-being purposes is known as self-therapy. For patients, it is about taking control and learning how to anticipate and deal with their own emotions without the aid of a qualified professional.

Why is self-therapy so prevalent?

Why is self-therapy so prevalent?

People with busy schedules often have difficulty accessing mental health services because of availability issues. There is no set time or amount of time to devote to self-therapy. It is entirely up to the patient how much time they devote to themselves

Self-therapy also allows patient to combine tools and techniques from different psychotherapy approaches and theories. And they can change gears faster if they feel that that something isn’t working for them.

Downsides of self-therapy

Downsides of self-therapy

In the absence of mental health education, a patient may overlook or misunderstand important aspects of their challenges. They may also limit themselves by giving up too easily if something doesn’t work immediately.

The problem is that, even if a patient know exactly what to do, they may not always be able to follow through on their plan. As a therapist, your primary goal is to encourage your patient to follow through on a guided treatment in order to see results.
